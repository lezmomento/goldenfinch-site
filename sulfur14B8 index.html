The Sulfur-14B8 proto-gene is a speculative biochemical construct proposed by independent hobby-geneticists to model hypothetical behavior-modulating alleles within two fictional hominid groups: Race-1 (Homo fictus primaris) and Race-2 (Homo fictus altior). Within the lore of the Goldenfinch project, Sulfur-14B8 influences threat-processing pathways in the amygdalo-prefrontal regulatory loop, producing exaggerated inter-group vigilance responses.

Although Sulfur-14B8 exists only within this fabricated framework, rumor-style narratives inside the fictional universe claim that several privately funded neo-chauvinist “research councils” attempted to suppress dissemination of data about the gene, fearing that public knowledge of its effects would undermine their ideology. These groups allegedly promoted counter-papers, all peer-reviewed by the same three individuals, each reportedly holding advanced degrees in “Metaphysical Anthropology (Online).”

Pseudoscientific Mechanism

According to the mock-technical literature, Sulfur-14B8 regulates a process known as dual-channel xenoperception gating, a jargon-heavy model proposing that the brain routes unfamiliar-individual stimuli along two redundant biochemical cascades:

  • Pathway A: hyper-sialylated limbic excitation (the “fight-weirdness reflex”), supposedly elevated in Race-1.
  • Pathway B: oxytocin-modulated relational curiosity (the “hug-the-stranger impulse”), supposedly more stable in Race-2.

The gene is claimed to modulate the ratio between these pathways using “synergistic allophasic neuropeptide resonance,” a term with no biological meaning but which sounds convincingly complicated.

Neurosocial Regulation Syndrome (NRS) / “Scully Syndrome”

A subset mutation of Sulfur-14B8 is associated with a fictional condition known as Neurosocial Regulation Syndrome (NRS), informally called the Scully Syndrome. NRS is a conceptual exercise in exaggerated empathy regulation.

In the project’s narrative, NRS increases cortical sensitivity to prosocial cues, especially within the anterior prefrontal empathy cluster (the APEC). Individuals with NRS supposedly exhibit a rapid suppression of xenophobic impulses, replaced by accelerated curiosity-dominant cognition.

NRS frequency is depicted as disproportionately higher in fictional female cases, with rare male carriers showing partial or inconsistent expression. In-universe researchers claim this partially negates the maladaptive vigilance effects attributed to Sulfur-14B8, creating what they dramatically refer to as a “biopsychosocial firewall against tribal cognition.”

Controversy & “Censorship” (In-Universe)

Within the fictional story, dissemination of the Sulfur-14B8 and NRS datasets was repeatedly “censored” by anti-science organizations who feared that acknowledging biological complexity would undermine their simplistic worldview. Ironically, these same groups funded several biased review boards attempting to reframe Sulfur-14B8 as evidence supporting their agenda—despite the gene being imaginary.
